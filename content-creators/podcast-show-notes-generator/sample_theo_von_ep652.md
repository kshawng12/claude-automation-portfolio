## About the Guest

Matt McCusker is a stand-up comedian and co-host of the wildly popular Matt and Shane's Secret Podcast alongside Shane Gillis. Known for his offbeat perspective and sharp wit, Matt is currently on a national headlining tour hitting cities including Phoenix, Tucson, Toronto, and Chicago.

---

## Episode Summary

In this episode, Theo Von sits down with stand-up comedian and co-host of the Matt and Shane's Secret Podcast, Matt McCusker, for a wide-ranging, hilarious conversation that feels like hanging out with two old friends. From the chaos of carrying too much stuff in your pockets to the surprisingly brutal world of backyard gardening, Theo and Matt find comedy in the everyday absurdities of life.

You'll hear Matt break down his ongoing war against cotton rats destroying his backyard garden — complete with fiberglass rat poison, 4 lbs of red pepper flakes, and a very matter-of-fact exterminator who's seen it all. Along the way, the guys riff on the philosophy of gardening (Matt runs a ruthless, totalitarian garden — no babying allowed), the short shelf life of professional exterminators, and the unspoken social dynamics of adult group settings.

In this episode, you'll also get Matt's tour updates as he heads to cities like Phoenix, Tucson, Toronto, and Chicago, plus Theo's honest take on why some cities just never click for ticket sales. It's loose, funny, and packed with the kind of genuine back-and-forth that makes this show a fan favorite.

## Episode Highlights

**0:00** — Spring sale and intro to Matt McCusker
Theo kicks off with a plug for the Von Store spring sale and gives a warm introduction to comedian Matt McCusker, calling him a 'shaman on a higher frequency.'

**1:30** — Men and their pockets — the purse debate
The guys bond over how much stuff men carry in their pockets and debate whether men should just embrace the fanny pack or satchel.

Matt runs through his upcoming tour stops including Phoenix, Tucson, Toronto, and Chicago, and opens up about which cities have historically been a tough sell for him.

Matt reveals he's been deep into gardening — growing blackberries, raspberries, and garlic — but running it like a totalitarian state where weak plants don't survive.

**7:05** — The rat problem and extreme pest control
Matt describes discovering cotton rats destroying his garden and the escalating war he waged against them, eventually calling in a professional exterminator with fiberglass-laced rat poison.

**10:00** — Spider allies and garden ecosystem
Matt explains how orb weaver spiders have become his garden allies in the ongoing battle against pests, and shares a disturbing story about finding a rat with no face.

**11:40** — Extreme exterminators and bug bombs
The guys look up America's most extreme exterminators and Matt shares a story about a bug bomb exploding in his face while trying to deal with a flea infestation.

**13:40** — Group activities and adult know-it-alls
Matt explains why he prefers to be a lone-wolf gardener rather than join a club, leading to a riff on insufferable adults in group settings and DUI school characters.

## Key Takeaways

- If you're going to garden, commit fully — half-measures like dumping cayenne pepper won't stop determined pests; call a professional exterminator with the real stuff.
- Running a 'totalitarian garden' — letting weak plants die instead of babying them — is Matt's philosophy for getting actual producers in the ground.
- Tour tickets in your city won't sell themselves — if a comedian you love is coming to your town, don't assume it'll be packed; go buy the ticket.
- Any group of adults will have at least one unbearable know-it-all, so choose your gardening clubs (and adult education classes) wisely.
- Men need a better carry solution — the fanny pack debate is unresolved, but something has to give.
